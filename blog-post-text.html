SEWING MACHINE OPERATING TRAINING

Dec 10, 2021 at 10:00 AM

Over the last decade, empowering women in poor communities has become a focal point in India. That is because about 50.7 million people live in extreme poverty in India, yet, as of 2019, only 20.7% of women in India are part of the labor force. Moreover, the country has recently seen a drop in its GDP from 6.1% to 5% and is attempting to recover from its uncertain economy. As a result, one solution that many nonprofit organizations and the government have recognized is investing in the population that is living under the poverty line. Specifically, many groups are empowering women in India through sewing. Today, being able to sew can be an acclaimed vocational skill


Sewing machine operator a 2 month full time course that teaches operation of an industrial sewing machine and different aspects of garment production. Garment production here started in 2019 with a simple range of skirts and palazzos, and gained momentum after the establishment of the community production center in January 2020. The team at the unit includes a full time designer, cutting and pattern master and a production supervisor. The team can produce a wide range of ladies, children and gents garments. The fabrics that our team can handle are cotton, chanderi and silk. About 200 women from the NGO participated and benefited from the training.
